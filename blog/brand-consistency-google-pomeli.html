Automating Brand Consistency with Google Pomeli

Gab · April 2026 · 5 min read

The biggest problem with using AI to generate content isn't quality—it's consistency. One day your AI writes like a pirate, the next day like a corporate lawyer. Your visuals shift from photorealistic to cartoonish.

This is "Brand Drift," and it kills trust.

Enter Google Pomeli. It's not a generator; it's a policeman.

How Pomeli Works

You feed Pomeli your "Business DNA": your hex codes, your font files, your tone of voice examples, and your "do not say" list. Pomeli creates a rule engine that sits between your generation tools and your publishing platforms.

Social Media Enforcement

Let's say I'm posting to Instagram and Facebook. I generate a caption with Gemini. Before I post, I run it through Pomeli.

Pomeli Warning: "This caption uses passive voice and three exclamation points. Your brand guidelines specify active voice and max one exclamation point."

It doesn't just flag it; it rewrites it. It ensures that my Facebook post sounds like me, even if I didn't write it.

Visual Consistency

For visuals, Pomeli is even more critical. If I'm generating images for an ad campaign, Pomeli checks the color palette. If the blue is slightly off-brand, it corrects it before I even see it.

When you are scaling content across multiple channels, you can't manually review everything. Google Pomeli gives you the confidence to automate without losing your soul.
