The Ultimate AI Video Workflow: Gemini, Google Flow, and YouTube

Gab · April 2026 · 6 min read

Video is the hardest content to produce. Or it used to be. The traditional workflow involves writing, storyboarding, filming, lighting, editing, color grading, and sound mixing. It's exhausting.

But the new workflow? It's just you, Gemini, and Google Flow.

Step 1: Scripting with Gemini

Don't start with a blank page. I feed Gemini a topic, for example: "The history of espresso machines." But I don't just ask for a script. I ask for a visual script.

"Write a 5-minute YouTube script about espresso machines. For each paragraph, describe the visual b-roll that should accompany it."

Gemini gives me the narration AND the prompt for the video generation. It does the thinking for two roles at once.

Step 2: Visualizing with Google Flow

This is where the magic happens. Google Flow is the best text-to-video tool I've used because it understands cinematic language. I take the visual descriptions from Gemini and paste them into Flow.

  • "Close up, slow motion, espresso dripping into a ceramic cup, golden crema, cinematic lighting."
  • "Wide shot, bustling Italian cafe in 1905, steam rising, sepia tone."

Flow generates these clips in seconds. They aren't just "usable" — they are beautiful. No stock footage subscriptions, no filming permits.

Step 3: Assembly and YouTube

I stitch the clips together (sometimes using a simple editor, sometimes automating it with Python), add a voiceover (often AI-generated if I'm feeling lazy, or my own if I want that personal touch), and upload to YouTube.

The result? A high-production-value documentary style video produced in an afternoon.

This workflow allows me to post 3x more often than my competitors who are still setting up softboxes.
