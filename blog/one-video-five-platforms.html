One Video, Five Platforms — The Content Repurposing Playbook

Gab · February 2026 · 8 min read

This is Part I of the content repurposing series. You'll learn how to take a single long-form video and turn it into native content for five different platforms — without recording anything new.

Most creators make the mistake of creating separate content for each platform. That's five times the work for marginally more reach. The smarter play is creating one piece of great content and reshaping it for everywhere else.

The One-to-Many Framework

Here's the idea: start with one long-form video (5-15 minutes). This is your anchor content. From it, you extract everything else.

What you get from one video:

  • YouTube — The full video lives here. This is your home base.
  • TikTok — 2-3 short clips (30-60 seconds each) pulled from the best moments
  • Instagram Reels — Same short clips, slightly reframed for Instagram's audience
  • Facebook — The full video or a 2-3 minute highlight reel for Facebook Watch
  • Reddit — A text post summarizing your key points with a link back to the video

One recording session. Five platforms. Each piece feels native because you're adapting format, not creating from scratch.

Step 1: Create the Anchor Video

Your anchor video should be structured with repurposing in mind. That means clear sections, strong standalone moments, and a logical flow that can be broken apart.

Structure it like this:

  1. Hook (0:00-0:30) — A compelling opening that works as a standalone clip
  2. Context (0:30-2:00) — Set up the problem or topic
  3. Main content (2:00-10:00) — 3-4 distinct sections, each making one clear point
  4. Summary (10:00-12:00) — Recap the key takeaways
  5. CTA (12:00-end) — Tell viewers what to do next

Each of those main content sections becomes a potential short-form clip. The hook becomes your TikTok opener. The summary becomes your Reddit post.

Step 2: Identify Your Clip Moments

Watch your anchor video and mark the timestamps where you said something that works in isolation. You're looking for:

  • Hot takes — Opinions that make people stop scrolling
  • Tips — Actionable advice that delivers value in 30 seconds
  • Stories — Quick anecdotes that illustrate a point
  • Results — Before/after moments, metrics, screenshots

You should be able to pull 3-5 clip-worthy moments from any well-structured 10-minute video. Not every moment needs to be a clip — pick the ones that would make someone say "wait, tell me more."

Step 3: Adapt for Each Platform

Each platform has different expectations. The content is the same, but the packaging changes.

YouTube (full video):

  • Upload the complete anchor video
  • Write an SEO-optimized title and description
  • Create a custom thumbnail
  • Add timestamps in the description for each section
  • Add end screens linking to related videos

TikTok (30-60 second clips):

  • Vertical format (9:16) — crop or reframe your footage
  • Add captions — most people watch without sound
  • Hook in the first 2 seconds — TikTok is ruthless about attention
  • End with a question or call to follow for more
  • Use 3-5 relevant hashtags

Instagram Reels (30-90 second clips):

  • Same clips as TikTok but remove the TikTok watermark
  • Instagram's audience skews slightly older — adjust your caption tone
  • Use the caption to add context the clip doesn't cover
  • Add to a relevant highlight on your profile

Facebook (2-3 minute highlights):

  • Combine your best 2-3 clips into a highlight reel
  • Or upload the full video — Facebook Watch rewards longer content
  • Write a conversational caption that asks a question
  • Post to relevant groups (if appropriate)

Reddit (text post with link):

  • Write a genuine text post summarizing your key points
  • Don't just drop a link — Reddit hates that
  • Share real value in the post itself, then mention the video for people who want more
  • Post to 2-3 relevant subreddits (not the same post everywhere)
  • Engage with comments — Reddit rewards participation

Step 4: Build a Repurposing Workflow

Once you know what you're doing, the whole process should take about an hour after your initial video is done.

The workflow:

  1. Record and edit your anchor video (this is the real work)
  2. Upload to YouTube
  3. While YouTube processes, cut 3 short clips for TikTok/Reels
  4. Upload clips to TikTok and Instagram
  5. Create a Facebook version (full or highlight)
  6. Write a Reddit text post
  7. Schedule everything to go live within the same 24-hour window

The key is doing it all in one session. If you say "I'll post to Reddit later," you won't. Batch the whole repurposing step right after you finish editing.

What This Looks Like in Practice

Let's say you run a small business and make a video called "5 Things I Wish I Knew Before Starting My Online Store." It's 12 minutes long.

From that one video, you get:

  • YouTube: The full 12-minute video with chapters
  • TikTok #1: "The one thing that almost killed my store" (45 seconds)
  • TikTok #2: "Stop spending money on X, do this instead" (35 seconds)
  • TikTok #3: "The tool that saved me 10 hours a week" (40 seconds)
  • Reels: Same 3 clips, re-uploaded natively
  • Facebook: 3-minute highlight reel combining the best parts
  • Reddit: Text post in r/ecommerce with your top 5 lessons

That's 10 pieces of content from one recording session. If you do this twice a week, you're posting 20 times across five platforms. That kind of consistency builds an audience fast.

Tools That Help

  • Video editing: CapCut (free, great for short-form), DaVinci Resolve (free, professional-grade)
  • Auto-captions: CapCut or Descript handle this well
  • Scheduling: Buffer or Later for scheduling posts across platforms
  • Analytics: Google Analytics for tracking which platform drives the most traffic back to your site
