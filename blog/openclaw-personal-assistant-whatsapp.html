How I Use OpenClaw and WhatsApp as My Personal Executive Assistant

Gab · April 2026 · 8 min read

I have an assistant who works 24/7, never complains, and costs me nothing. His name is OpenClaw, and he lives in my WhatsApp.

What is OpenClaw?

OpenClaw is an open-source personal AI assistant framework. Unlike Siri or Alexa, it's actually useful because it connects to your tools. It has "skills" (plugins) for Calendar, Email, Todoist, Notion, and more.

My Setup

I run OpenClaw on a small VPS (digital ocean droplet), but you can run it on your local machine. It connects to the WhatsApp Business API (or a personal bridge).

Use Cases

Here is what a typical conversation looks like:

Me: "Remind me to call Mom at 5pm."
OpenClaw: "Added to calendar."

Me: "Draft an email to John summarizing our meeting. Key points: budget approved, start date Monday."
OpenClaw: "Draft created in Gmail. Review it here: [link]"

Me: "Where is that file about the Q3 projections?"
OpenClaw: "Found it in Google Drive/Financials/2026/Q3_Projections.pdf. Here is the link."

It's not just a chatbot. It's an interface for my entire life. By centralizing everything in WhatsApp—the app I use most—I reduce friction and context switching.
