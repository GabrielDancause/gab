The Viral Content Machine: Automating Social with Google Pomeli

Gab · February 2026 · 7 min read

In Part I, we built a business for $0/month. But a business without customers is just a hobby. To get customers in 2026, you need attention.

The problem? Creating high-quality content for TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and Facebook takes hours. The solution? Build a machine that does it for you.

Here is my automated "Viral Content Machine" workflow, powered by the latest tools from Google.

Step 1: Idea Mining with Reddit

You don't need to be creative; you just need to be observant. The best content answers questions people are already asking.

The Tool: Reddit.

Go to subreddits related to your niche (e.g., r/Entrepreneur, r/WebDev, r/SaaS). Sort by "Top - This Week". Look for the posts with the most engagement. These are your content pillars.

Example: Someone asks "Is it worth learning to code in 2026?" and gets 500 comments. That's your video topic.

Step 2: Scripting with Gemini

Once you have the topic, you need a script that hooks the viewer in the first 3 seconds.

The Tool: Gemini (Google's Advanced Model).

I feed the Reddit thread into Gemini and ask it to extract the key arguments, then structure them into a 60-second video script. Gemini's 2M token context window is perfect for analyzing hundreds of comments to find the most resonant points.

PROMPT: "Analyze this Reddit thread. Identify the top 3 contrasting opinions. Write a 60-second video script that presents these views and concludes with a data-backed recommendation. Use a provocative hook."

Step 3: The "Brand DNA" with Google Pomeli

This is the game-changer. In the past, AI content felt generic. Google Pomeli fixes that.

The Tool: Google Pomeli.

Pomeli acts as a "Brand Identity API". It analyzes your previous best-performing videos to understand your tone, visual style, and pacing. It then enforces these rules on any new content you generate.

I feed my Gemini script into Pomeli. It tweaks the language to sound more "like me" and generates a storyboard that matches my brand's aesthetic (e.g., dark mode, neon accents, fast cuts).

Step 4: Video Generation with Google Flow

Now we need visuals. We aren't filming anything.

The Tool: Google Flow (powered by Veo).

Pomeli sends the storyboard directly to Google Flow. Flow generates the high-definition video clips. If the script calls for "a futuristic city with flying cars," Flow creates it. If it needs "a coder typing frantically in a dark room," Flow generates it.

The integration between Pomeli and Flow ensures the lighting and color grading match my brand guidelines perfectly.

Step 5: Omni-Channel Distribution

One video asset, four platforms. You must be everywhere.

The Tools: TikTok, Instagram (Reels), Facebook (Reels), YouTube (Shorts).

Each platform has a slightly different vibe, but the core content works across all of them. I use a simple automation (often via Make or Zapier, or a custom Python script similar to the one in Part II) to upload the final video to all four platforms simultaneously.

  • TikTok: For reach and virality.
  • Instagram: For converting viewers into followers/customers.
  • Facebook: Surprisingly high reach for older demographics.
  • YouTube Shorts: For long-tail search traffic.

The Result

This workflow turns 15 minutes of Reddit research into a high-production-value video distributed to millions of potential viewers.

  • Input: Reddit trend + Gemini script.
  • Processing: Google Pomeli (Brand DNA) + Google Flow (Visuals).
  • Output: 4x Social Posts.

You aren't spending hours editing in Premiere Pro. You're spending your time finding the best ideas. The machine handles the rest.
