AbbVie (ABBV)

Company Analysis Investment Wiki — Fundamentals
AbbVie Inc. (ABBV) is a research-based biopharmaceutical company that spun off from Abbott Laboratories in 2013. It is best known for Humira, the world's best-selling drug for nearly a decade. AbbVie has since diversified its portfolio through the acquisition of Allergan and the development of new blockbusters in immunology and oncology. It is a "Dividend King," recognized for its reliable cash flows and shareholder returns.

1.0 Business Overview

AbbVie operates as a single global business with a focus on four primary therapeutic areas. The integration of Allergan in 2020 significantly expanded its portfolio into aesthetics and neuroscience.

Immunology

The core powerhouse. This segment includes:

  • Humira: Once the primary revenue driver, now facing biosimilar competition. Sales are declining but remain significant.
  • Skyrizi & Rinvoq: The successors. These newer drugs are rapidly growing and are projected to eventually surpass Humira's peak revenue combined.

Oncology

A key growth driver focusing on blood cancers. Major drugs include Imbruvica and Venclexta.

Neuroscience

Treatments for migraine, Parkinson's, and psychiatric disorders. Key products include Botox Therapeutic, Vraylar, and Qulipta.

Aesthetics

Acquired via Allergan. This cash-pay (non-insurance) business includes Botox Cosmetic and the Juvederm collection of fillers.

Competitive Moat

AbbVie possesses a "Wide Moat" characterized by:

  • Intellectual Property: A fortress of patents protecting key drugs like Skyrizi and Rinvoq, extending exclusivity well into the 2030s.
  • High Switching Costs: Particularly in aesthetics (Botox), where brand loyalty and physician training create significant barriers to entry.
  • R&D Scale: Billions in annual R&D spending ensure a continuous pipeline of next-generation therapies to replace aging blockbusters.

2.0 Financial Performance

Cash Flow & Dividends

AbbVie is a "Dividend King," having raised its dividend for over 50 consecutive years (including its time as part of Abbott). The company generates massive free cash flow, which supports:

  • A high dividend payout ratio.
  • Debt reduction (deleveraging from the Allergan acquisition).
  • Continued R&D investment.

4.0 Risks

Patent Cliff (LOE)

Loss of Exclusivity (LOE) is the biggest risk. Humira lost US patent protection in 2023, leading to an influx of cheaper biosimilars and a sharp decline in revenue for that drug.

Drug Pricing Regulation

The U.S. Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) allows Medicare to negotiate prices for certain high-spend drugs. Imbruvica has already been selected for negotiation, and others may follow.

Pipeline Execution

Pharmaceutical companies must constantly invent new drugs to replace aging ones. Failures in clinical trials can severely impact future growth prospects.
