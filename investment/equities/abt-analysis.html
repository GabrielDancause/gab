Abbott Laboratories (ABT)

Company Analysis Investment Wiki — Fundamentals
Abbott Laboratories (ABT) is a diversified global healthcare company with a portfolio spanning medical devices, diagnostics, nutrition, and branded generic pharmaceuticals. Known for its steady growth and over 50 years of consecutive dividend increases (a Dividend King), Abbott combines the stability of consumer staples with the innovation of a medical technology leader.

1.0 Business Overview

Abbott operates through four primary segments, providing a balanced revenue stream that mitigates risk in any single area.

Medical Devices

The primary growth engine (~45% of revenue). Key products include:

  • FreeStyle Libre: A market-leading Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM) system for diabetes management.
  • Structural Heart: Devices like MitraClip for heart valve repair.
  • Rhythm Management: Pacemakers and ICDs.

Diagnostics

A major revenue contributor (~25% of revenue). Includes:

  • Core Laboratory: The Alinity family of systems used in hospitals and labs.
  • Rapid Diagnostics: Point-of-care testing (e.g., BinaxNOW for COVID-19, though volumes are normalizing).

Nutrition

A steady cash generator (~18% of revenue). Includes:

  • Pediatric: Similac (infant formula) and Pediasure.
  • Adult: Ensure and Glucerna.

Established Pharmaceuticals (EPD)

Focuses on branded generics in emerging markets (~12% of revenue). This segment provides access to high-growth regions like India, Russia, and Latin America, where branded generics command loyalty and pricing power.

3.0 Valuation

Abbott typically trades at a premium to the broader market and some healthcare peers due to its "best-in-class" quality, diversification, and dividend history.

4.0 Risks

Litigation

Like many healthcare giants, Abbott faces legal risks. Specifically, lawsuits regarding Necrotizing Enterocolitis (NEC) in premature infants fed with cow's milk-based formula (Similac) have created an overhang on the stock.

Foreign Exchange (FX)

With a significant portion of sales coming from outside the U.S. (especially in Established Pharmaceuticals), a strong U.S. dollar can negatively impact reported revenue and earnings.

Pricing Pressure

Global healthcare systems are under pressure to reduce costs. Initiatives like China's Volume-Based Procurement (VBP) can compress margins for medical devices and pharmaceuticals in key markets.
