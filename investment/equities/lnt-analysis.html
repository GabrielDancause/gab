Alliant Energy (LNT)

Company Analysis Investment Wiki — Fundamentals
Alliant Energy Corporation (LNT) is a regulated public utility holding company headquartered in Madison, Wisconsin. Through its subsidiaries, Interstate Power and Light Company (IPL) and Wisconsin Power and Light Company (WPL), it provides electric and natural gas service to approximately 995,000 electric and 425,000 natural gas customers in Iowa and Wisconsin. The company is known for its steady earnings growth, consistent dividend payments, and strategic transition toward renewable energy generation.

1.0 Business Overview

Alliant Energy operates as a fully regulated utility, meaning its rates and returns are determined by the Iowa Utilities Board (IUB) and the Public Service Commission of Wisconsin (PSCW). This regulatory framework provides predictable, though capped, returns.

Electric Utility

The primary driver of the business, generating approximately 85% of earnings. Alliant is actively transitioning its generation portfolio away from coal and toward wind and solar under its "Clean Energy Blueprint." This transition not only reduces carbon emissions but also grows the rate base through capital investment.

Gas Utility

Generates approximately 15% of earnings. This segment focuses on natural gas distribution and infrastructure modernization in Iowa and Wisconsin.

Competitive Moat

As a regulated monopoly, Alliant Energy benefits from exclusive service territories, creating a significant barrier to entry. Its moat is reinforced by the capital-intensive nature of utility infrastructure and economies of scale.

2.0 Financial Performance

Earnings Growth

Management targets a long-term earnings per share (EPS) growth rate of 5-7%. This growth is primarily driven by rate base expansion—investing capital into renewable energy projects and grid modernization, which allows the company to earn a regulated return on a larger asset base.

Dividends

Alliant Energy is a "Dividend Achiever," having increased its dividend for over 20 consecutive years. The company targets a payout ratio of 60-70%, which is considered safe and sustainable for a regulated utility. This makes LNT attractive for income-focused investors.

3.0 Valuation

Utilities are often valued as "bond proxies" due to their stable cash flows and dividends. LNT typically trades at a slight premium to the sector average due to its favorable regulatory jurisdictions and renewable growth pipeline.

4.0 Risks

Regulatory Risk

The most significant risk for any utility. Adverse rulings from the IUB or PSCW regarding allowed ROE, capital structure, or cost recovery can directly impact earnings. Fortunately, both Iowa and Wisconsin are generally considered constructive regulatory environments.

Interest Rate Sensitivity

Utilities are capital-intensive and carry significant debt. Rising interest rates increase borrowing costs, potentially compressing margins. Additionally, high rates make "risk-free" Treasury bonds more attractive relative to utility dividends, putting downward pressure on the stock price.

Renewable Execution

The "Clean Energy Blueprint" involves massive capital deployment into solar and wind projects. Delays, cost overruns, or supply chain issues could hinder the company's ability to grow its rate base as projected.
